'DaVinci Sleep', or just nuts?

I don't know if Leonardo DaVinci actually used anything like this sleep pattern, but both Seinfeld and TV Guide told me he did, so it must be true. My hope is to be able to completely ignore the normal 24 hour day cycle an d apply this unique sleep pattern to my everyday life. Of course, this whole idea is based solely on the fact that I once had a psychology teacher who mentioned that the human body was not meant to work on a 24 hour cycle.

Information

I plan to work on a 26 hour day. This means that the cycle will repeat itself every other week. Within that 'day', I plan to have six sleep cycles (SC). Each SC will consist of being awake for 3:50 and sleeping for 0:30. This comes out to a tota l of 3 hours of sleep for each 'day'. When taken to a solar day (24 hours), I will be getting approximately 2.76 hours of sleep per day. Many people will say that this is not enough to keep yourself running.

I feel that keeping good eating habits as well as setting a regimented excercise schedule will allow me to continue this schedule and eventually adapt to it so that I have more energy while on it than I do currently. If I continue to follow this sc hedule I think my body will recognize the pattern and eventually I will become tired during the sleep part of my schedule and alert during the awake part. I think this is how sleep has always worked, but people generally do not keep themselves on a stric t enough schedule for their bodies to find the pattern and therefore they become lethargic.

Certain activites must be set up within the schedule so as not to lose the regularity which had been previously set by the sun. I will eat a small meal directly before each sleep cycle (six meals a day), showers come after the first sleep cycle and oral hygene after the second and fifth sleep cycles. I will also set up an excercise regimen which will be posted here when it is complete. Unfortunately, I can not schedule classes to fit within the schedule, but I hope to balance my excercise and pra ctice time (I am a guitarist) as well as homework, so the load falls pretty evenly throughout the day. This means that thwe bulk of my non-class activities will take place during the night. My bi-weekly structural schedule is also on-line with a compact graphic version, and a faster, but more awkward text version. Hopefully, I will document each day in a six column table with each column representing one sleep cycle.
