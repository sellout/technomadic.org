Diesel, Mark II, Version 1

Well, there used to be a lot of crap on this page begging people to help me find what I was looking for in a laptop. Unfortunately, what I want isn't in a laptop. Laptops are too rigid, not enough to play with. I want to get my hands on the hardw are and tear the machine up, so I decided to build one. Originally, I had chosen not to build one, because I needed something portable and laptops aren't just something you can build without a lot money and friends in the industry. Well, then I started looking at things that seemed pretty cool, the first being the Twiddler. I was looking at these extremely portable options, thinking, wow, maybe I can get something more portable than a laptop. Well, once I got to the MIT Medialab site I was hooked. Not only are these things more portable than laptops, they are cheaper, I get to put whatever I want in it (up to my maximum carrying capacity) and everyone seems to be using Linux, which just goes against the grain of commercialism all together. So, here it is, I made my decision to become a cyborg in the MIT sense of the word.

First things being first, I had to figure out how to build a wearable computer and my first stop was the Lizzy design. I also joined the wearable mailing list while I was hanging around the site. Now, I know what you're all thinking: 'Lizzy' is a female name and 'Diesel' is almost definitely male. Well, that's true, but given a few modifications the problem is solved. Right away I figured that to make this computer ooze testostorone, I had to find some new hardware, but I didn't know where to look . . . at least not until I ran into the PC-Clone UNIX Hardware Buyer's Guide and Jeff Moe's Build Your Own PC page. Here I learned all about cool stuff like why DOS sucks and what a 'parity bit' is (it's sort of like the X and Y chromosomes). So leaving here I felt a lot more educated about hardware and a lot less ready to tackle my own system. At this point I felt I needed to enlist some help from the tech-heads on the mailing list, but I needed to have answers to questions very quickly as I would be designing and building the whole thing within the space of a month.

For those of you who aren't aware of it, ICQ is one of the greatest programs ever created. I decided to use ICQ to get the answers I needed in real time. I could set up a chat room with whichever tech-heads were online and work on the machine as we chatted. This is where the project is at right now and hopefully I will soon be constructing my wonderful machine with their help.

I know, I know, I'm leaving you hanging without telling you some very important aspects of the machine. Well, unfortunately, I don't have any pictures yet, but I'll give you a description of the finished product. Here's the paint job: It's going to be glossy black, with the flames like you see on the old Chevy cars - not realistic, but rounded and bright red. The front and back panels of the case will be chrome and the center of the case will be overlayed with the girl from the side of the Memphis Belle. I don't know how I'm going to do the HUD yet, so if you have any design ideas (hopefully ones that go along with the main case), feel free to send me a message on the Web Pager below.
