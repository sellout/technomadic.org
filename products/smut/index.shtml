Single Motor Unit Training

Single Motor Unit Training (SMUT) is new to me. Apparently it existed in the 70s as some form of medical treatment to regain use of digits and limbs. The field died when researchers it was not possible to train enough of them. It is being reborn as a method of computer input.

Training a few of these takes only a couple hours. With twelve to sixteen of them, you could control an incredible amount of information. Not just single letters, but whole words and other things as well. The rate of input for them is around 240 words per minute, which is much greater than any typist can reach. Also, many SMUs aren't used at all, which makes it easy to use them for input whilst not interrupting any other activities.

Imagine being able to type by having a band around your upper arm, no one would be able to see it and you could type at incredible speeds. Taking dictation or keeping notes of what someone is saying couldn't be any easier.

Of course, there are two other major pieces of a wearable computer: the display and CPU. These too are are going through major changes, although historically the input device has been the most difficult to implement.
