Products

Hermes
This is a minimal wearable computer that lets you take the power of a workstation with you. By using a remote connection, you can make use of all of your home computers applications while on the road.
Armboard
Wouldn't voice input be great? But then, everyone can hear what you're "typing". How do you fix that? Well, now you can "type" hands-free and silently, at rates up to 240 wpm.
Contact Lens Display
HMDs are so bulky. When you're wearing it, your social life hits ground zero. Now it's on your eye. The display is hidden in a [non-]prescription contact lens and covers your entire field of vision.
O Server
Although the Contact Lens Display will work with a standard X server, you waste a good bit of the available display area. The GPLed O Server fixes that, making full use of the visual field.

Outside Projects

These are developments in wearable computers that are being worked on by other organizations. Although we are not doing it ourselves, we find them interesting and will eventually incorporate these technologies into our products.

Personal Area Network (PAN)
Wires are always getting in the way of wearable computers. This technology, being developed by IBM and others, turns your entire body into a "wet wire", allowing different devices on your body to communicate wirelessly. You can also initiate data transfer merely by shaking hands with them. This is the network of the future.
